At a glance

Quick Facts

The four numbers that shape every decision you make on the test.

📋 Format

A two-hour objective test, 60 multiple-choice questions split evenly across biology, chemistry, and physics.

⏱ Pacing

≈ 2 minutes per question. Lead with your strongest subject, flag hard items, and use the full clock.

🎯 What wins

Accuracy over volume. Protect easy points, skip blind guesses, and specialize for subject awards.

Points & penalties

Scoring Strategy

Because wrong answers are penalized, every blank is a deliberate choice. Here's the math that should drive it.

Score = 6 × correct − 2 × wrong
Blank answers count as 0

🎯 How points work

+6
Correct
0
Blank
−2
Wrong
  • A blank answer is worth 0 — it never hurts you.
  • A wrong answer loses 2 points (an 8-point swing versus correct).
  • Random guessing can hurt your score. Don't fill bubbles just to fill them.

🧠 Guessing Rule (5 answer choices)

Choices eliminatedDecision
0 eliminatedUsually skip
1 eliminatedClose to break-even
2+ eliminatedUsually worth considering
High confidenceAnswer
EV = 6p − 2(1 − p) = 8p − 2
Guessing becomes positive when p > 25%  (p = your chance of being correct)
⚠️
This is a strategy rule, not a UIL rule. Each student's personal guessing threshold should be based on their own practice data — the platform tracks how your guesses actually pay off.
What you can bring

Allowed Materials

Bring the right calculator and a backup. When in doubt, defer to the official approved list.

✅ Allowed

  • Approved scientific calculator only
  • Up to two approved calculators
  • Spare batteries
  • Silent timing device with no audible signal
  • Scratch paper provided by contest officials
  • Writing on the test booklet and scratch paper

🚫 Not allowed

  • Cell phones
  • Smart watches
  • Tablets
  • Internet-connected devices
  • Music players or headphones
  • Using the answer sheet for calculations
  • Opening the test before instructed

🧮 Approved calculator examples

  • Casio FX-260
  • Sharp EL-501X
  • TI-30Xa
  • TI-30XIIS
  • Simple 4-function calculator — if no approved model is available
🎒
Bring the correct calculator to every practice and every meet. Practicing with an illegal calculator can create habits that do not transfer to the real contest. Verify your exact model against the current UIL-approved Science calculator list each season.
In the room

Contest Room Rules

Know these cold so nothing on meet day surprises you.

1
Wait for the start signal
Do not open the test until told to begin.
2
Use your contestant number
Write the assigned contestant number on the test and answer sheet. Do not write your name or school on the answer sheet.
3
Use the answer sheet correctly
Only answers written on the answer sheet count.
4
Write clearly
Use clear capital letters. If graders cannot read an answer, it can be marked wrong.
5
Know the time rules
A 30-minute notification is given. No other oral time warnings are required — use a silent timing device.
6
Stop immediately
When time is called, stop working.
7
Stay official
Do not discuss or share confidential contest material before official release.
How you advance

How Advancement Works

There are multiple ways to matter in UIL Science — individual, subject specialist, and team.

🥇
Overall Individual
The highest total scores place individually and advance to the next level.
🔬
Subject Top Scorer
Biology, Chemistry, and Physics are also scored separately — the top scorer in each subject advances too.
👥
Team Score
A school's team score depends on its top overall performers. The best team isn't four identical students — strong subject specialists can raise the whole team.

What Great Competitors Do Differently

The habits that separate strong scorers from the rest.

⏭
They skip intelligently
They do not turn uncertain guesses into negative points.
🛡
They protect easy points
They do not miss basic formulas, units, vocabulary, or graph-reading questions.
🔬
They specialize
A student can become extremely valuable by dominating Biology, Chemistry, or Physics.
📓
They review mistakes
They fix the reason they missed the question — not just the answer.
⚙️
They practice under real rules
Same calculator, same time pressure, same scoring, every time.
